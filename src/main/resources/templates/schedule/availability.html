What Your Scores Mean

Depression: Measures dysphoria, hopelessness, devaluation of life, self-deprecation, lack of interest/involvement, anhedonia, and inertia.
Anxiety: Assesses autonomic arousal, skeletal muscle effects, situational anxiety, and subjective experience of anxious affect.
Stress: Evaluates difficulty relaxing, nervous arousal, and being easily upset/agitated, irritable/over-reactive, and impatient.
